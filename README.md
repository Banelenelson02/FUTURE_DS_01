# Business Sales Performance Analytics (Task 1)

## Objective
The goal of this project is to analyze business sales data to identify revenue trends, top-selling products, high-value categories, and regional performance[cite: 22].

## Key Insights

**1. Revenue Trend Over Time**
* Sales experienced steady growth throughout 2011, culminating in a massive spike in November. 
* There is a sharp drop in December, which likely indicates incomplete data for that month rather than a sudden drop in actual sales.

**2. Top-Selling Products**
* The top two products by a wide margin are "PAPER CRAFT, LITTLE BIRDIE" and "MEDIUM CERAMIC TOP STORAGE JAR", both selling around 80,000 units. 

**3. Revenue by Price Category**
* **Mid-Range items ($2 - $10)** are the primary revenue driver, contributing **51.8%** of total sales.
* **Budget items (<$2)** make up a significant **40.7%**, showing the business relies heavily on high-volume, low-cost goods.
* **Premium items (>$10)** make up only **7.5%** of revenue.

**4. Regional Performance**
* Excluding the home market (UK), the top three international markets are the **Netherlands, EIRE (Ireland), and Germany**.

## Actionable Recommendations
1. **Inventory Management:** Prepare inventory for "Paper Craft" and "Ceramic Top Storage Jars" well in advance of the Q4 holiday spike to prevent stockouts.
2. **Marketing Focus:** Since mid-range and budget items drive over 90% of revenue, marketing campaigns should focus on bundling these low-cost items to increase the average order value.
3. **International Expansion:** Consider localized marketing or reduced shipping thresholds for the Netherlands, EIRE, and Germany to further boost these strong secondary markets.
